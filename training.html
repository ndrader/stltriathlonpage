Workout Guide

How to Use the Workout Guide:

There will be 4 Phases of training leading up to the St. Louis Triathlon, lasting 4 month or 16 weeks. The phases include: Base phase (6 weeks), build phase (5 weeks), specialty phase (4 weeks), and taper (1 week). For each phase, there will be a weekly template which will show you the general weekly structure; there will also be weekly updates which is to be used with the general template in order to progress your fitness. Follow your current training phase’ template for every week of that training phase, then read the weekly updates for changes to your training from week to week.

How to Adjust for Missed Workouts:

  • If you missed 1-4 days, skip the days that you missed, and continue with the plan.
  • If you missed 5-7 days, disregard the main set for the workout and swim or bike or run easy for 2 days, then continue with plan as instructed.
  • If you miss 1 week or more, repeat the previous week of training, and skip the last week of the training phase that you are on.

Base Training Phase (6 weeks) Overview and Goals

  • Improve cardiovascular fitness
  • Increase bone density and joint durability to handle the build phase training
  • Training intensity for most workouts will be moderate
  • Improve body composition (If needed)
  • Develop the habit of consistency

Appendix

  1. RPE stands for rate of perceived exertion on a scale from 1-10. 1 as in very easy, 10 for the effort of a 100 meter sprint. A 5 or 6 generally indicates an easy or moderate aerobic effort or “all day pace”, and 8 or 9 is race pace.
  2. VO2max is the maximum amount of oxygen your body can intake in the units of ml/kg/min, it is an indicator for fitness, can be only measured in lab settings. World champion athletes have VO2max from 70-93 ml/kg/min, while the average untrained person has VO2max of 20-40 ml/kg/min. Sled dogs can have VO2max from 175 (untrained) to 300 (trained). Workouts consisting of hard 1-6 minute intervals RPE 9-9.5 with equal recovery are targeted to improved VO2 max.
  3. Lactate threshold is the intensity of exercise where lactate in your body can accumulate faster than your blood’s ability to remove. High concentration of lactate in your body is acidic, which causes the soreness sensation during exercise that makes you want to stop. Workout intervals consisting of 7-20mins RPE 8 – 9 with shorter recovery are aimed to improve lactate threshold.
  4. Strides are close to sprint effort runs of RPE 9-10 for a short duration of time: 10-20 seconds. Goal of strides are to improve efficiency through running faster. Since it is short, it also does not create a lot of fatigue. Strides can be completed anytime even if not instructed.
  5. Brick is a type of workout which you do one immediately after the other. Most common brick is the bike/run brick, in which you run immediately coming off the bike.
  6. Tempo runs are harder efforts above easy and endurance, it is below the efforts of lactate threshold, but can occasional brush it. It is a run that improves aerobic fitness without creating a lot of fatigue
  7. Descends in a swim set means you get faster with each interval. 4 x 50m descends would mean your first 50m would be the slowest, and last 50m would be the quickest.
